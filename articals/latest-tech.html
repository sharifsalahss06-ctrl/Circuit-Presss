The iPhone 17: Is Apple's AI Worth Your Student Loan?

By Salah Al Din Sharif, Tech Editor
October 30, 2025

Alright, the iPhone 17 is here, and let's be real: it looks pretty much just like the iPhone 16. So, is this just another "S" year cash grab, or is the new "Apple Intelligence" stuff actually legit? I’ve had it for a month, using both the 17 Pro and the standard 17, and honestly, the answer is... complicated. Here's the breakdown on whether it's worth your cash.

The Good Stuff (What's Actually New?)

Siri Finally Got a Glow-Up

The A19 Bionic chip in the Pro is wild. It has a 32-core Neural Engine, which is just a fancy way of saying **Siri isn't useless anymore**. You can now ask it complex stuff like, "Summarize my last three emails from my tutor and draft a reply," and it *actually does it*. The best part? It's all on-device, so it’s fast and private. The standard 17 gets last year's Pro chip, which is still fast, but the Pro model is on another level.

The Pro Camera is Nuts (And the Standard One is Solid)

The 17 Pro camera is a beast. The Ultra-Wide lens is finally 48MP, so your wide shots aren't soft and grainy anymore. But the real flex is **4K Spatial Video**. This is Apple's 3D video for the Vision Pro. Let's be honest, 99% of us will never use this, but it’s cool, I guess? More importantly for us, the **standard iPhone 17 gets the 48MP main camera** from last year's Pro. This is a huge win. Your Insta stories and night-out photos will look incredibly crisp, and you get a proper 2x zoom without a third lens.

The *Real* MVP: 45W Charging

This is it. This is the best feature. Apple *finally* gave the Pro models **45W wired charging**. I can get from 0 to 50% in about 20 minutes. This is an actual game-changer when you've forgotten to charge overnight and have a 9 AM lecture. The Pro Max battery is also a two-day beast. This feature alone is almost worth the upgrade.

The Bad Stuff (The Big Scam)

That 60Hz Screen... Still?

Okay, let's talk about the scam. The **standard iPhone 17, in late 2025, still has a 60Hz screen**. It's a joke. My friend's three-year-old Android phone has 120Hz. It’s visibly choppy, and it's Apple's way of disrespecting base-model buyers to force an upgrade to the Pro. The 17 Pro’s 120Hz ProMotion screen is amazing, but it shouldn't be an "Pro" feature anymore. It's just stingy.

The Verdict: Should You Buy It?

So, should you use your student loan on this?

For the iPhone 17 Pro: If you're a content creator, a serious photographer, or one of the five people who own a Vision Pro, yes. The 4K Spatial Video, 48MP Ultra-Wide, and 45W charging are massive. If you have an iPhone 15 Pro or older, it's a huge leap.

For the iPhone 17: This is a tough one. The 48MP camera is a fantastic upgrade. But that 60Hz screen is just disrespectful. If you're coming from an iPhone 12 or 13, you'll feel the difference. But if you're not desperate, you might be better off finding a cheap iPhone 16 Pro.

Honestly, this year feels like the Pro models finally broke away. The 17 Pro feels like the future (AI, 45W charging), while the standard 17 feels like Apple is still holding back.
